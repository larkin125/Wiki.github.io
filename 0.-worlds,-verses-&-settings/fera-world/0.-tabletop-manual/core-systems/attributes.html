Attributes

Every character in Fera possesses six core Attributes that represent their natural capabilities. Attributes affect almost every action a character performs, from swinging a sword and climbing a cliff to negotiating with a noble or resisting magical influence.

Attributes typically range from 1 to 10, with 5 representing the capability of an average adult.

Attributes provide modifiers to Skill Checks, Combat Actions, Recovery Rolls, and various other mechanics throughout the game.

Might

Might represents a character's physical strength, lifting capability, and ability to exert force.

Characters with high Might can wield heavy weapons effectively, carry greater loads, force open doors, and overpower opponents in physical contests.

Might is commonly used for:

  • Melee attacks
  • Grappling
  • Lifting and carrying
  • Breaking objects
  • Physical labour
  • Athletic feats of strength

A blacksmith, dockworker, or veteran soldier will often possess a high Might score.

Agility

Agility represents coordination, reflexes, balance, and fine motor control.

Characters with high Agility react quickly to danger, move gracefully, and perform delicate physical tasks with precision.

Agility is commonly used for:

  • Dodging attacks
  • Stealth
  • Acrobatics
  • Ranged combat
  • Lockpicking
  • Balance and coordination

Thieves, hunters, duelists, and scouts often rely heavily upon Agility.

Endurance

Endurance represents toughness, stamina, resistance to injury, and overall physical resilience.

Characters with high Endurance can endure hardship, survive harsh conditions, resist disease, and continue fighting despite pain and exhaustion.

Endurance is commonly used for:

  • Resisting disease
  • Surviving blood loss
  • Recovering from wounds
  • Enduring fatigue
  • Withstanding environmental hazards
  • Maintaining physical exertion
  • Maintaining Magical Aether Usage in conjunction with Willpower

Labourers, soldiers, explorers, and Mages often develop high Endurance.

Knowledge

Knowledge represents learning, memory, reasoning, technical expertise, and understanding of the world.

Characters with high Knowledge are well educated, observant, and capable of mastering complex subjects.

Knowledge is commonly used for:

  • Medicine
  • Engineering
  • Alchemy
  • Historical lore
  • Investigation
  • Research
  • Navigation

Physicians, scholars, artificers, and engineers rely heavily upon Knowledge.

Willpower

Willpower represents mental resilience, discipline, determination, and force of character.

Characters with high Willpower can endure fear, pain, temptation, and supernatural influence without faltering.

Willpower is commonly used for:

  • Resisting fear
  • Maintaining concentration
  • Enduring torture
  • Resisting magical influence
  • Mental recovery
  • Remaining calm under pressure
    • Maintaining Magical Aether Usage in conjunction with Endurance

Monks, officers, priests, and experienced veterans often possess strong Willpower.

Presence

Presence represents confidence, charisma, leadership, social awareness, and personal influence.

Characters with high Presence naturally command attention and can inspire loyalty, fear, respect, or admiration.

Presence is commonly used for:

  • Persuasion
  • Negotiation
  • Leadership
  • Intimidation
  • Performance
  • Deception
  • Public speaking

Nobles, merchants, politicians, and military commanders frequently possess high Presence.

Attribute Limits

Most ordinary individuals possess Attribute scores between 3 and 7.

Scores of 8 or higher are uncommon and indicate exceptional talent or extensive development.

Scores of 10 represent the limits of normal mortal capability.

Certain races, magical effects, artefacts, or supernatural powers may temporarily or permanently exceed these limits.

Attribute Checks

When a character attempts an action not covered by a Skill, the Game Master may call for an Attribute Check.

To make an Attribute Check:

Roll 1d20 and add the relevant Attribute Modifier.

If the result equals or exceeds the Difficulty set by the Game Master, the action succeeds.

Example:

A character attempts to force open a jammed iron gate.

The Game Master calls for a Might Check with a Difficulty of 15.

The character possesses Might 7 (+2 Modifier).

The player rolls 12 on a d20.

12 + 2 = 14

The attempt fails.
