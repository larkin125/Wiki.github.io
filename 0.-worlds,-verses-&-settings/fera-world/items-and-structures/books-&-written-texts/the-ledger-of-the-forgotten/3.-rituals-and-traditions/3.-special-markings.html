1. Special Markings

Special Markings - Descriptions and Drawings

The markings carried by the Children of the Abandoned are more than simple symbols. They are powerful representations of each member's journey, sacrifice, and dedication. Each mark is carefully crafted and engraved in a distinct manner to serve as both a personal reminder and a public declaration of commitment to the collective. Below are the descriptions of each marking and their method of drawing:

The Mark of Silence

Description:
The Mark of Silence is a symbol of undisturbed discipline. It is a reminder that true strength comes not from what is spoken, but from the silence kept in moments of trials or provocation. This mark represents complete emotional control and a steadfast commitment to unity. Those who wear it are regarded as paragons of patience and tranquility, able to speak through actions rather than words.

How It Is Drawn:
The Mark of Silence is drawn across the back of the left hand with a single, horizontal slash of ink or scar. The line should be thin but firm, signifying clarity of resolve. It must be straight, with no curves or adornments, symbolizing pure discipline and an unbroken commitment to silence.

The slash should be black in color, representing the unwavering shadow of restraint. For members who’ve earned it, the marking is done by an elder, ceremoniously and with the understanding of its deeper meaning.

The Shadow’s Mantle

Description:
The Shadow's Mantle represents unity forged through sacrifice and selflessness. It is awarded to those who have performed acts of great unity and devotion to the fellowship. This mark is a manifestation of the bond that holds the Children of the Abandoned together, symbolizing those who have gone above and beyond to act for the collective good.

How It Is Drawn:
The Shadow's Mantle takes the form of three interconnected rings, each of progressively larger diameter, radiating outward in a spiral formation. These rings are hand-stitched or embroidered in shadowy black or deep grey thread on the cloak or armor of the wearer.

For those who wear the mantle, the rings are drawn in a fluid motion that connects them in a continuous loop, reinforcing the unity in action. The interconnected nature of the rings represents how one act of selflessness ripples outward to benefit the whole fellowship.

The Circle of Brotherhood

Description:
The Circle of Brotherhood marks those chosen to lead or serve as a pillar of support within the group. This symbol signifies trust, responsibility, and the bonds formed within the fellowship. It is a mark of leadership, held only by those who exemplify wisdom, humility, and service in their actions.

How It Is Drawn:
The Circle of Brotherhood is represented as a thick circle, filled in with intricate braids of silver, bronze, or dark steel. The braids themselves should be patterned in an interlocking fashion, representing both unity and the interconnectedness of every member in the group. The circle should be bold, sturdy, and firm, symbolizing strength within leadership and shared responsibility.

It is always worn across the left shoulder or across the back of the wearer in a cloth representation, woven tightly, embodying the trust and strength they hold within the fellowship. The mark may also appear as an engraved insignia on their personal armor.

The Sign of the Night

Description:
The Sign of the Night represents a member’s spiritual journey through solitude, self-reflection, and their eventual return to the group as a true part of the collective. It is a mark earned after a long, arduous trial undertaken in complete darkness, where members must confront their fears and doubts in isolation before returning renewed.

How It Is Drawn:
The Sign of the Night is a simple but deep marking: a single, vertical slash drawn under the lower edge of the helmet. This line should be dark black, drawn without flourish, as a constant reminder of the isolation endured during the trial. It should appear to “glow” beneath the helmet as a subtle mark only visible to the most observant of the Children, serving to symbolize the unseen inner work of the individual.

The mark is meant to be slight, ethereal, and almost imperceptible. Its simplicity reflects the purity of the trial and the true humble nature of the member’s return from darkness.

Additional Considerations for Markings

  1. Placement:
    Markings are often placed on easily visible parts of the body to demonstrate the wearer's ongoing commitment and sacrifice for the collective good. Common locations include the hand, the shoulder, the upper back, or beneath the lower edge of the helmet.
  2. Renewal and Integrity:
    It is not uncommon for the markings to require renewal every few seasons to maintain their meaning and vibrancy. The Child who bears these symbols must constantly strive to embody the values they represent. Should they falter, it is expected that they undergo a form of purification to maintain the integrity of their markings.
  3. Recognition and Reverence:
    Upon seeing the markings, fellow Children immediately recognize the deep personal sacrifice of the one who bears them. These marks, while individual in nature, are seen as symbols of shared dedication and commitment to the group's survival. When a member with one of these marks speaks or acts, their words and deeds are met with deep respect, as their actions carry the weight of the entire fellowship.
  4. The Path of Achievement:
    These special markings are seen as an evolving journey. A member may wear the Mark of Silence to begin with, but as they serve and grow in their understanding, they may earn further markings along the way, each symbolizing greater feats and deeper levels of unity. Those who have achieved the most may wear multiple symbols, each telling a unique aspect of their journey and reminding them and others that true strength always remains rooted in the group.

Final Thoughts on Markings:

The act of drawing, wearing, and displaying these markings is an incredibly important process within the Children of the Abandoned. They act not as awards or symbols of glory, but as constant reminders of the responsibilities each member carries in their commitment to the collective. Whether a mark signifies discipline, selflessness, leadership, spiritual growth, or resilience, it embodies a truth: These markings are not merely for the individual—they are for the group, the unity above all else.
