Summary

The Kalihenate is the ancient imperial dominion of the Asalak, a civilisation built not upon borders or bloodlines, but upon possession. At its height, the Kalihenate stretched across great swathes of old Umissia (Continent), its courts populated not by nobles but by carefully chosen hosts; warriors, scholars, chieftains, and kings, all silently steered by the parasitic rulers within them.

The Kalihenate’s power rested on three pillars:

  • Subjugation, through the infiltration of political and religious elites.
  • Exaltation, granting chosen hosts extraordinary abilities.
  • Unity, maintained through the Asalak’s collective consciousness, which allowed instantaneous coordination across vast distances.

Though they were eventually overthrown by the rising Üstün Empire (Nation) and enslaved as living tools, remnants of the Kalihenate endured. Driven into the deepest jungles of Pawhye (Continent), the Asalak rebuilt in secrecy. There, hidden in ancient temple-cities smothered by vines and mist, the Kalihenate survives as a shadow empire, patient, calculating, and ever seeking new hosts through whom it might reclaim the world that once bowed to it.

The Servants of the Kalihenate

The Host-Guard serve as the elite warriors and enforcers of the Kalihenate; hosts chosen not only for strength, but for obedience, presence, and symbolic value. Each one carries an Asalak symbiote, granting them heightened senses, rapid reflexes, and a near-fanatical loyalty. To outsiders, they appear almost divine; to the Kalihenate, they are simply perfected vessels.
