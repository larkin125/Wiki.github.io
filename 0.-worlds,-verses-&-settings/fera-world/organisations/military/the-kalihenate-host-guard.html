The Kalihenate Host-Guard

Tags

#FeraWorld

Summary

The Host-Guard serve as the elite warriors and enforcers of The Kalihenate; hosts chosen not only for strength, but for obedience, presence, and symbolic value. Each one carries an Asalak symbiote, granting them heightened senses, rapid reflexes, and a near-fanatical loyalty. To outsiders, they appear almost divine; to The Kalihenate, they are simply perfected vessels.

Their armour is instantly recognisable.

Armour Design & Aesthetic

The Helmets

Their helmets are towering, stylised visors shaped not like literal serpents or birds, but echoing the mythic grandeur of such creatures.

  • The Kahl-Masks
    Sleek, elongated helms with sweeping crests that rise high above the head, resembling ancient god-faces, smooth, expressionless, unnervingly calm. Thin slits glow faintly with the Asalak symbiote’s colour.
  • The Shyr-Crests
    Wide, angular headpieces with flared side-panels and a crown-like ridge, evoking a regal, predatory presence. These are typically worn by commanders or ceremonial guards.

The motifs are abstract, not literal: sweeping lines, flowing curvature, geometric ridges, designed to make the wearer look inhuman, flawless, untouchable.

Armour Body

The armour of the Host-Guard is crafted of lacquered bronze, volcanic glass-ceramic, obsidian plating, or darkened living-metal alloys. Each set is form-fitting, ritualistic, and deeply symbolic.

  • Layered plates shaped like overlapping scales or feathers, again abstract rather than naturalistic
  • High gorgets and broad, angular pauldrons that exaggerate the wearer’s silhouette
  • Ribbed breastplates engraved with Kalihenate glyphs of unity, obedience, and ascension
  • Heavy greaves and vambraces reinforced to endure immense physical strain

Their armour is designed not just for battle, but for awe. When a phalanx of Host-Guard marches, the overlapping plates produce a deep, resonant chime—almost musical, intentionally unnerving.

Weapons

While weapons vary by rank and ritual, they commonly wield:

  • Khal-staves; polearms that discharge bursts of aetheric force
  • Ritual great spears with obsidian or crystal tips
  • Hook-blades designed to disable rather than kill, ideal for capturing new hosts

Each weapon is built to exist as both a tool of war and a holy symbol.

Hierarchy

1. The Crested (Commanders)

Wear towering Shyr-Crests.

Often partially merged with their symbiote for faster communication.

2. The Masked (Elite)

Wear the sleek, elongated Kahl-Masks.

Serve as bodyguards to Asalak overlords and temple guardians.

3. The Vessels (Rank-and-file hosts)

Less ornate armour, still intimidating smooth helms, fewer engravings.
Used for mass deployments or guarding outer territories.

Behaviour & Purpose

The Host-Guard operate with eerie synchronicity, often moving or reacting before an order is fully spoken, guided through shared impulses transmitted by their Asalak parasites. Their presence near an Asalak overlord is suffocating; silent, disciplined, unshakably loyal.

They are not merely soldiers.

They are living symbols of The Kalihenate’s power, walking reminders of what happens when a people become vessels for an empire of parasites who consider themselves gods.
