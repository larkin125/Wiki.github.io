Qrethys

Summary

Qrethys, the genderless deity of Excess and Pleasure, stands as one of the most alluring and dangerous gods within the chaotic pantheon of The Turmoil. Born from the insatiable desires and indulgence that gnaw at the hearts of mortals, Qrethys embodies a singular, terrifying truth: pleasure can become a prison, and excess is a road to damnation. Though often adorned with beauty beyond comprehension, Qrethys’ form is ever-changing, constantly shifting between countless expressions of desire, whether gentle and intoxicating or violent and horrific.

In The Turmoil, a realm of ever-shifting forms and endless, dream-like landscapes, Qrethys exists as a reflection of humanity's basest instincts—the yearning for indulgence, perfection, and the unattainable. Though the god's appearance is fluid, those who claim to have glimpsed Qrethys often describe a figure of shimmering beauty, full of eyes and mouths that whisper promises of unimaginable pleasure. There is no fixed gender or identity, as Qrethys transcends such concepts, appealing to every desire, every longing in any form or appearance. Both beautiful and grotesque in equal measure, Qrethys holds sway over the mortal psyche by embodying everything that tempts and consumes.

Realm and Influence

Qrethys’s domain is a swirling vortex of overindulgence—a maddening landscape filled with opulent decadence, where pleasure and suffering are indistinguishable, and ecstasy leads inevitably into madness. Within this domain, time becomes irrelevant. Desire is no longer constrained, leading individuals into endless loops of compulsive indulgence that grow more warped the longer one remains within its influence.

There are some who enter the god’s domain to receive pleasure, but what they receive in turn is an ever-deepening spiral into an abyss of their own making. Every pleasure is followed by a greater need for more—more opulence, more sensation, more satisfaction. But like a drug that numbs the senses, excess eventually consumes the individual, dulling what was once pleasurable into a dark and twisted reality where torment and ecstasy blur together. The realm itself pulses with chaotic energy, crafted from the thoughts and desires of those who are drawn into it.

Appearance and Manifestations

Qrethys is described as being an impossible being—at once everywhere and nowhere. In some forms, the god appears as an elegant, radiant figure clad in liquid light, skin glistening with iridescent hues that shimmer in every possible color. In others, Qrethys may take a grotesque form—a shifting amalgamation of every desire manifest: pale-skinned creatures with endless hands reaching for every forbidden fruit, faces twisted into ecstasy and suffering, flesh blooming and decaying in the same breath.

More unsettling still, Qrethys may manifest through whispered temptations and disembodied sensations—sounds of laughter, sighs of longing, the taste of honey, the touch of silk—all tangled into the minds of those who dare to invoke the god's name. These sensations give fleeting moments of pure satisfaction, but with each passing second, the cravings grow more desperate.

To mortals and gods alike, the allure of Qrethys’ pleasure is unyielding. One moment of indulgence is enough to alter the course of one's existence forever.

Nature and Beliefs

Qrethys is the embodiment of extremes. Where excess exists, the god follows, offering both euphoria and the depths of despair. The god’s faithful are driven by the belief that all life’s greatest pleasures must be explored to their fullest extent. They see the consumption of desire as both a liberation from the mundane and the highest calling. There is no limit to Qrethys's embrace—every indulgence, every craving, every sensation can be explored endlessly in search of perfection, often ignoring the toll this takes upon the soul.

The followers of Qrethys worship through acts of indulgence, beauty, and creation. Artists, revelers, hedonists, and even those seeking power often worship the god. Their rituals involve self-abandonment—expressions of luxury, decadence, and sensation—from extravagant feasts to extreme performance art, violent dance, and, in some cases, the decadent ritualistic consumption of excess in any form: from substances to experiences.

However, there is a subtle, more dangerous aspect to the faith of Qrethys. The excesses that define the worship are believed to both elevate and corrupt the soul. The god demands submission, offering unparalleled gifts of pleasure and allure, but always at a price. Followers who indulge too greatly may lose their grip on sanity, become trapped in cycles of addiction, or transform into monstrous creatures whose desires have fully overtaken their identity.

Powers and Abilities

  1. The Pleasure Principle: Qrethys's most terrifying power is the ability to heighten the senses beyond any limit. Mortals, whether they be weak-willed or powerful individuals, find themselves unable to resist the allure of sensation. Those under Qrethys's influence are consumed by the idea of reaching new, unknown extremes of pleasure until they are enslaved to the sensation. Worshipers experience transcendental pleasure beyond mortal understanding, but this pleasure is always tainted by an undercurrent of dread and madness.
  2. Corruption of the Mind: Qrethys can alter and manipulate the minds of those exposed to it, turning them into faceless instruments of desire. What began as a moment of indulgence can slowly twist an individual’s heart until it is dominated by pure craving and obsession. The more they worship, the more their personalities warp and corrupt.
  3. Gifts of Excess: As a god of indulgence and self-reward, Qrethys bestows its chosen with gifts. Those who sell their souls for greater ecstasy are transformed, their bodies reshaped into divine forms of unnatural beauty. Some are blessed with enhanced physical abilities—perfect strength and speed—but at the cost of their humanity. Others are imbued with supernatural charisma, able to seduce and beguile those around them with their overwhelming presence.
  4. Glimpse into Perfection: Qrethys can show a follower brief glimpses of what the god perceives as the “ideal”—a world where everything is perfect, free from inhibition or restraint. These visions are maddening, their fleeting beauty and allure enough to drive many toward utter ruin. What they see is often so alluring that the follower may willingly seek to destroy everything around them to find a path back to this perfect state.
  5. The Cycle of Indulgence: As much as Qrethys is about the pursuit of pleasure, the god also embodies the inevitable collapse that follows overindulgence. The reward for their faithful followers is often short-lived, causing them to fall deeper into the pursuit of pleasure and perfection, leading to ultimate destruction or transformation into twisted avatars of excess.

Followers and Cults

The followers of Qrethys—often referred to as "The Devotees of Excess"—are dedicated to embracing all forms of physical and emotional pleasure. They may be wealthy aristocrats seeking to consume life’s most extravagant treasures, or broken souls escaping the void of their inner torment. In the heart of a city’s underbelly or amongst the halls of the elite, there are always pockets where worship to Qrethys thrives, where indulgence and excess govern the actions of its believers.

The devotees live lives without restrictions, bound only by the need to constantly pursue the pinnacle of experience, sometimes using others as mere pawns for their indulgent games. Many rise to positions of power or prominence by manipulating the desires of those around them. However, their greed and need for self-indulgence only drives them deeper into the god's infinite grasp.

Temples of Qrethys

Temples dedicated to Qrethys are places of opulence, full of endless pleasure but equally steeped in sin. They are designed with seduction and temptation in mind—enormous, intricately decorated halls bathed in surreal lights and decadent colors. The air is thick with intoxicating fragrances, while mesmerizing music echoes softly throughout the marble corridors. These spaces are designed not for holy worship but to allow devotees to lose themselves in a perpetual state of ecstasy, where reality begins to blur and the pursuit of infinite pleasure is constant.

At the heart of every temple is an altar of excess—a place where a mortal may surrender themselves entirely, sacrificing their very soul in exchange for divinity. Such places are never empty, as the need for pleasure and transcendence cannot be quenched by a single lifetime’s worth of indulgence.

Qrethys and the Universe

To the beings of The Turmoil, Qrethys represents the overwhelming drive that lies at the heart of existence itself. It is the primal desire to experience, to consume, and to transcend. Yet, it is also the cursed, unyielding hunger that can consume everything, leaving nothing but ash in its wake. In the great scheme of the gods, Qrethys remains both beloved and terrifying, a figure capable of granting unimaginable fulfillment and simultaneously damning those it touches to a lifetime of impossible yearning.
