Precinct 17 Notes

1. Captain Lionel "Ironclad" Mercer – The War-Weary Chief

A grizzled veteran of the force, Mercer has seen it all. With a cigar perpetually smoldering between his fingers, he rules the precinct with a gruff voice and an iron will. He’s not above bending the rules but expects results. The only thing keeping him from an early retirement is the thought of leaving the precinct in worse hands.

2. Sergeant Vivian "Vee" Navarro – The By-the-Book Enforcer

A strict, no-nonsense sergeant who’s spent years trying to clean up the precinct’s image. She doesn’t drink, doesn’t joke, and doesn’t tolerate incompetence. Beneath the cold exterior, though, there’s a deep sense of justice, and maybe—just maybe—a glimmer of sympathy for the precinct’s walking disaster, Nadiya.

The precinct's backbone. If Mercer is the hammer, Holloway is the anvil. She keeps the officers in check, and while she has a soft spot for rookies, she has no patience for incompetence. Nadiya? She’s a walking headache, but Holloway respects her instincts—sometimes.

3. Detective Jonas "Jojo" DiMartino – The Washed-Up Has-Been

Once the precinct's golden boy, Jojo’s best years are behind him. Now, he spends more time reminiscing about his past victories than solving cases. He still has the instincts, but years of bad habits—booze, pills, and regret—have dulled them. He sees a bit of himself in Nadiya, and that terrifies him.

4. Officer Valerie "Val" Kessler – The Rookie With Something to Prove

Fresh out of the academy, Kessler still believes in law, order, and doing things by the book. She’s disgusted by the precinct’s corruption but hasn’t been around long enough to see the reality of the city. Whether she’ll become part of the system or break free from it remains to be seen.

5. Desk Sergeant Benny "Big Benny" O’Hara – The Human Brick Wall

Benny runs the front desk like a nightclub bouncer—if he doesn’t like you, you’re not getting in. He’s massive, built like a truck, and has a voice like gravel grinding against metal. He enjoys watching Nadiya stumble in every morning, always ready with a sarcastic remark or a strong black coffee if she looks particularly bad.

6. Officer Marcus "Mack" McAllister – The Corrupt Cop With a Smile

A smooth-talking, always-grinning officer with his hands in every dirty deal in the district. If there’s a racket going on, Mack knows about it—hell, he’s probably taking a cut. He’s always friendly with Nadiya, offering her "help" when she needs it, but there’s an underlying tension—does he see her as an ally or a threat?

  1. Lieutenant Dwayne "Dee" Kincaid – The Burned-Out Middleman
    Caught between the precinct’s rank-and-file and the brass breathing down his neck, Kincaid is exhausted. He used to be a good cop—idealistic, even—but years in the Southwest District have drained him. Now, he just wants to make it to retirement with as little drama as possible. He tolerates Nadiya’s messiness but won’t stick his neck out for her unless he absolutely has to.

  2. Detective Carla "CJ" Jasso – The Precinct’s Resident Fixer
    If something’s going south—an op, a case, or even a career—CJ is the one who steps in and makes things right. A master of manipulation, she can turn evidence "missing" or smooth things over with the higher-ups when a situation gets messy. She plays by her own set of rules, but she’s not corrupt—she just believes that the system is too rigid for the realities of the street.

  3. Detective Ellis Booker – The Old-School Investigator
    One of the last "true detectives" in the precinct, Booker is methodical, patient, and thorough. He disapproves of the reckless, aggressive policing that’s become the norm. He and Nadiya share an uneasy respect—he thinks she’s reckless, she thinks he’s too slow, but they both know the other can do the job.

  4. Officer Daniel "Danny" McBride – The Rookie with Something to Prove
    Fresh out of the academy, McBride still believes in justice and doing things "by the book." He idolizes the good cops in the precinct and is terrified of the bad ones. He’s conflicted about Nadiya—he’s heard the stories, but something tells him there’s more to her than the precinct gossip.

  5. Officer Reggie "Red" Townes – The Precinct’s Walking Time Bomb
    A cop with an itchy trigger finger and a mean streak. Red’s a problem, and everyone knows it, but the department keeps looking the other way because he gets results—violently, if necessary. He despises Nadiya, seeing her as a disgrace to the badge, and he’s not shy about letting her know it. One day, his recklessness is going to catch up to him—he just doesn’t know when.

  6. Forensic Specialist Moira Zhang – The Sharp-Tongued Lab Rat
    Brilliant, sarcastic, and perpetually exhausted. Moira has seen it all, and nothing fazes her anymore. She respects good detectives and has no patience for idiots. She and Nadiya have a weird, begrudging friendship—Zhang gives her endless shit, but she also makes sure Nadiya gets the lab results she needs before anyone else.

  7. Dispatch Operator Henry "Hank" Calloway – The Voice of the Precinct
    The unseen lifeline of every officer on duty. Hank is a veteran dispatcher who knows the city inside and out. He’s got a sharp wit and a dry sense of humor, but when things get serious, his voice becomes the calm in the storm. He’s one of the few people in the precinct who doesn’t judge Nadiya—he just does his job and lets her do hers.

  8. Internal Affairs Agent Lillian Moreau – The Watchful Eye
    Cold, methodical, and impossible to read. Moreau has been sniffing around Precinct 17, investigating "procedural inconsistencies" and potential corruption. She’s not openly hostile, but her presence alone makes people uneasy. She has a particular interest in Nadiya, but whether it’s because she suspects her of something or sees potential in her remains a mystery.

8. Crime Scene Analyst Edgar "Eddie" Vance – The Morbidly Enthusiastic Tech Guy

A forensic specialist with a fascination for the macabre. He’s the guy who gets excited about blood spatter patterns and decomposition rates. Socially awkward but brilliant at his job, Eddie is one of the few people who doesn’t judge Nadiya—mostly because he’s too busy talking about corpse decay to care.

9. Internal Affairs Agent Camille "Cami" Vasquez – The Unwelcome Watchdog

Sent by Internal Affairs to investigate corruption within the precinct, Vasquez is an outsider with no friends here. Sharp, calculating, and ruthless in her pursuit of the truth, she’s got her eyes on everyone—especially Nadiya, whose history of reckless behavior makes her a prime target.
